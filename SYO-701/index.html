    FUNDAMENTALS OF SECURITY

    Threats and Vulnerabilities

    Threat: harm, lost, damage or compromise info or system

    • Natural Disasters
    • Cyber-attacks
    • Data Integrity breaches
    • Disclosure of confidential information

    Vulnerability: Any weakness in the system design or implementation They come from internal factors

    • Software bugs
    • Misconfigured software
    • Improperly protected network devices
    • Missing security patches
    • Lack of physical security

    but it can be prevent from being exploited

    • Mitigating
    • Transferring, avoiding or accepting the risk (becomes a risk management decision)

    Remember Neme: If I have:

    • Threat + No vulnerability = No Risk
    • Vulnerability + No threat = No Risk

    Risk Management: Finding different ways to minimize the likelihood (probabilitie) of an outcome occurring and achieve the desired outcomes.

    Cybersecurity is the continuity of services to the end users, maintaining the overall security of the systems and protect the organization from data breaches by minimizing the vulnerabilities or mitigating the risk it this impact.

    Confidentiality

    Protect the information from unauthorized access and disclosure (Could be an individual, entity or process).

    Keeping information secret and safe from prying eyes, only access who they need to access.

    3 main reasons of Confidentiality

    1. Protect personal privacy
    2. Maintain a business advantage
    3. Achieve regulatory compliance (PII, PHI, Various types of financial data
    4. PII: Personal identifiable information, PHI: Protected health info.

    How to maintain the Confidentiality

    1. Encryption
    2. Access Controls
    3. Data Masking
    4. Physical Security Measures
    5. Training and awareness

    Security Controls Categories

    Multifaceted layers of protection to form a security posture

    Main function to protect Confidentiality, Integrity and Availability

    1. Technical Controls: 

      HW and SW implemented to manage and reduce risk **(Firewalls, Encryption, Intrusion Detection Systems).**

    2. Managerial Controls or Administrative Controls:

      Strategic planning and governance side of security align to the business goal and risk tolerance **(conduct a risk assessments, security policies, training programs, incident response strategies for make decision and keep informed the organization in the same security page).

    3. Operational Controls:

      Procedures and measures designed to protect data  on a day to day basis most of them governed by internal processes and human actions **(change the passwd, backup procedures, account reviews, user training programs).** this can be ongoing process to ensure the security.

    4. Physical Controls: 

      Tangible, real-world measures taken to protect assets (digital and non-digital). Like**( Shredding of sensitive documents, Security guards, Locking doors).**

    Security Control Types

    This will depend of the specific threat o vulnerability to address

    1.- Preventive Controls: proactive measures, build foundation

    proactive measures implemented to thwart potential security threats or breaches before the inciden occur **(firewall).**

    2.- Deterrent Controls: discourage threats- warnings

    Aim to discourage potential attackers by making the effort seem less appealing or more challenging. **( Warning Signs, banners to indicate the monitoring is occurring)**

    3.- Detective Controls: detection and notification, watchful eye.

    Monitor and alert to malicious activities as the occur or shortly thereafter. ** (Network: IDS (Intrusion-Detection-System).**

    4.- Corrective Controls: jumping in emergencies

    Mitigate any potential damage and restore the system to their normal state.**(mitigate the vulnerabilities).*

    5.- Compensating Controls: backups and mitigations

    Alternative measure implemented when primary security controls are not feasible or effective *(plan b of security)***example:*** in a test online: 3 layers of security network: my phone, cable dsl, wifi)

    6.- Directive Controls: guide the entire process

        Policy or documentation that set the standards for behavior in the organization. **(policy how employs use company-owned IT assets).**

    Zero Trust Never trust, always verify...

        this mean we can not just trust to the network perimeter to protect the system as a main line of defense, with deperimeterization (common in cloud and remote work and adoption of new thecnologies)we are put a lot or risk into the organization that is why we need to focus on: strong authentication, data encryption access controls per user/devices, zero trust architecture.

    Zero Trust: demands verify for every device, user and transaction within the network, regardless of its origin (it doesn't mater where it comes from: inside or outside the network.

    1. Control Plane: is the brain (making decisions)

    Adaptive Identity, threat scope reduction, policy-driven access control, secured zones with Policy Engine and Policy Administrator.

    2. Data Plane: is the hands (doing the actual work) 

    Subject/System and Policy Enforcement Point

    1. Control Plane: responsible for defining, managing, and enforcing the policies related to user and system access.

    1 Adaptive Identity: validation into account user behavior, device, location and more.

2 Threat scope reduction: Limit he access only for the need work tasks, avoid risk and compromise credentials.

3 Policy-driven access control: Enforce user access policies based on their roles and responsibilities.

4 Secured zones: Isolated environments within a network that are designed to house sensitive data.

    Control Planes uses: Policy Engine and Policy Administrator to makes security decisions like:

    • is the user allowed to access this app?
    • is this devices secure enough to connect?
    • Should this request be blocked or allowed?
      1 Policy Engine: cross-references the access request with its predefined policies.

2 Policy Administrator:  Used to establish and manage the access policies, aligning with the organization security protocols and business objectives.

    2. Data Plane: data traffic flows: like emails, files or apps requests between users, devices and services.

    1.- Subject/System: the objective is verify the authenticity of the subject or system. Refers to the individual or entity attempting to gain access: employee, a workstation or software application..

2.- Policy Enforcement Point:  Allow or restrict access act as a gatekeeper to sensitive areas of systems and networks.
