    FUNDAMENTALS OF SECURITY

    Threats and Vulnerabilities

    Threat: harm, lost, damage or compromise info or system

    • Natural Disasters
    • Cyber-attacks
    • Data Integrity breaches
    • Disclosure of confidential information

    Vulnerability: Any weakness in the system design or implementation They come from internal factors

    • Software bugs
    • Misconfigured software
    • Improperly protected network devices
    • Missing security patches
    • Lack of physical security

    but it can be prevent from being exploited

    • Mitigating
    • Transferring, avoiding or accepting the risk (becomes a risk management decision)

    Remember Neme: If I have:

    • Threat + No vulnerability = No Risk
    • Vulnerability + No threat = No Risk

    Risk Management: Finding different ways to minimize the likelihood of an outcome occurring and achieve the desired outcomes.

    Cybersecurity is the continuity of services to the end users, maintaining the overall security of the systems and protect the organization from data breaches by minimizing the vulnerabilities or mitigating the risk it this impact.

    Confidentiality

    Protect the information from unauthorized access and disclosure (Could be an individual, entity or process).

    Keeping information secret and safe from prying eyes, only access who they need to access.

    3 main reasons of Confidentiality

    1. Protect personal privacy
    2. Maintain a business advantage
    3. Achieve regulatory compliance (PII, PHI, Various types of financial data
    4. PII: Personal identifiable information, PHI: Protected health info.

    How to maintain the Confidentiality

    1. Encryption
    2. Access Controls
    3. Data Masking
    4. Physical Security Measures
    5. Training and awareness

    Security Controls Categories

    Multifaceted layers of protection to form a security posture

    Main function to protect Confidentiality, Integrity and Availability 1. Technical Controls: HW and SW implemented to manage and reduce risk (Firewalls, Encryption, Intrusion Detection Systems).

    1. Managerial Controls or Administrative Controls: Strategic planning and governance side of security align to the business goal and risk tolerance (conduct a risk assessments, security policies, training programs, incident response strategies for make decision and keep informed the organization in the same security page).

    2. Operational Controls: Procedures and measures designed to protect data on a day to day basis most of them governed by internal processes and human actions (change the passwd, backup procedures, account reviews, user training programs). this can be ongoing process to ensure the security.

    3. Physical Controls: Tangible, real-world measures taken to protect assets (digital and non-digital). Like( Shredding of sensitive documents, Security guards, Locking doors).

    Security Control Types

    this will depend of the specific threat o vulnerability to address

    1. Preventive Controls
    2. Deterrent Controls
    3. Detective Controls
    4. Corrective Controls
    5. Compensating Controls
    6. Directive Controls

    1.- Preventive Controls: proactive measures implemented to thwart potential security threats or breaches before the inciden occur (firewall).
